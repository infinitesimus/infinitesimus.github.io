About Me

When I was young, I remember sitting in a brightly-lit room attending art class. Beside me, there was a large canvas hanging on the wall that laid out a Fulfillment Wheel. It depicted a circle partitioned into seven parts—each part representing seven key areas of personal fulfillment: intellectual, career, social, physical, family, financial, and spiritual.

I didn't pay too much attention to it then, only gazing at it when my mind wandered off in class. My only thought was that it would be great to have all parts in equal proportions. This seemingly tacky wheel has continued to make an impact on me to this very day.

It has lead me to value and admire balance and diversity in ourselves.

Over the years, it has made me want to become a polymath.

"Show, don't tell."

You are probably reading this because you don't know me well. I'm sorry to say I'm not good at describing myself so you will only get so much out of reading this.

I like to do things. So instead of telling you who I am, I hope my projects and companies will express me better than me writing about myself.

Not wanting to hang you out to dry, here are some snippets about me:

  • I enjoy the beauty and oddity in life.
  • Growing up, I spent half of my time playing outdoors and half of my time reading at the library.
  • I traveled around the world for 3 years while running my business.
  • Marathoned in North Korea.
  • I play tennis and golf.
  • Utilitarian and anarcho-capitalist.
